The pattern

We’ve heard this story before.

01

It starts with a spreadsheet.

Someone builds a stock tracker in Excel. It works. Then you add a second warehouse. Then a Shopify store. Then Amazon. Now you’ve got four systems that have never agreed on a single number.

02

Then the mistakes start.

An order ships from the wrong location. A customer buys something out of stock. A return lands and nobody updates anything. Each one costs you a customer you already paid to acquire.

03

Then you stop trusting your own data.

You walk the floor to count stock because the system can’t be trusted. You over-order because you’d rather have dead stock than a stockout. Your 45-day lead times don’t forgive guesswork.

This isn’t a software problem. It’s what happens when you’ve outgrown the tools you started with.

Still independently owned

While our competitors were being acquired, we were getting better at what we do.

Brightpearl was acquired by Sage. Cin7 sold to EQT Partners. Linnworks went to Marlin Equity. Orderwise joined the Forterro roll-up. Every major player in our market has been through the same private equity playbook: raise prices, cut support, extract margin.

Stok.ly has not been acquired. We are backed by Midven and run by the people who built the platform. Our customers have a first-name relationship with the people who answer the phone. We intend to keep it that way.
